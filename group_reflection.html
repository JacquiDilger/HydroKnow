Individual Reflections

Will

What an incredible group of individuals to work with for this group assignment! Being my first Uni subject, I will admit that I was on the nervous side for how the whole group work aspect would work online, but now, on the other side, I must admit that I am blown away by how well we all worked together and the quality of the work that we produced. Everyone was super collaborative, helpful and hardworking! This meant that whenever we delegated tasks out, you could trust that each person would complete each task, and to a very high standard as well!

If there were something that we, as a group, could improve, I would say be more on top of the meeting agendas and minutes. I didn’t involve myself much in those documents. However, in future, I will intend to make more of an effort to contribute to working on those documents in the lead up to each meeting and during them, to ensure that all sessions run smoothly and that task isn’t left to just one member, as it was many times during this assignment.

It surprised me how well we were able to work together, often in group work, disagreements happen, and it is hard to get solid collaborative work done. Still, everyone was so easygoing and open to new ideas. This meant we all worked together really cleanly and effectively, which created a healthy group environment, and I looked forward to catching up with everyone in our weekly meetings.

A key takeaway from this group work is reinforcing how crucial communication is and how important it is to stay on top of the tasks you are given. I felt I let the team down by not getting the raspberry pi fully up and running, despite myself being charged with that task, so I will endeavour to, in future group work, make sure that when I commit to something, I also produce the work expected of me, or, earlier on, let the group know that I don’t think I will be able to complete the task and come up with a compromise, which is what ended up happening in this assignment.

Jarrad

Jeremy

With the major assignments now close to completion, I’m excited to be taking the next step in my IT studies, but sad to be farewelling our group. Group Reflection

Just as Jarrad said, we really couldn’t have asked for a better first group to be a part of for the major assignments of this course.

One thing that we would all change is implementing a task planner from the beginning. Throughout assignment 3 we had a bit of confusion in regards to what each of us were working on. By using a task planner, we would have been able to keep track of tasks in more of an organised fashion rather than rely on our next meeting to ask one another. We could have also used some of the Team’s built-in functions to help organise and delegate tasks. This is now something we will all reflect on in another group product in the future.

From the beginning of assignment 3 to the end, we all gave ourselves more time to complete tasks. After we each completed tasks, we then began a new one. By doing this, we were not forced to complete so many components toward the due date, similar to what we did with assignment 2.

As Will mentioned, we could have been a little more active in the meeting agendas and minutes as a group. We mostly found that by mentioning one another in comments in the chat or on the team's home page was adequate. This communication method worked quite well for us and perhaps for that reason we continued to use these methods.

Each of us contributed equally to either GitHub, Canva or the written content. It really helped having the content from the previous assignment already at a good standard. It also helped having us all contribute to the GitHub and Canva pages and pick up from where we left off previously. Where possible, we applied each other's strengths to certain aspects of the project which worked really well. The running joke throughout the assignment was that Jacqui was going to complete the entire assignment herself. The joke is still running, so thanks Jacqui, the backbone of this project!

Jeremy raised a good point in regards to our team having so little experience with programming, web design, medias production, and hardware but we really did tackle each of these elements effectively between the 5 of us. We are all very much in agreeance with Jeremy’s statement, it has increased each of our confidence that we were otherwise intimidated by.

As Jacqui mentioned in her reflection, each of us were constantly working on different aspects of the assignment and we each left a clear trail of the work produced. There was consistent contribution from every member whether this was on GitHub, our Canva report, creating artefacts or writing the report itself.

Throughout the assignment, which Renee briefly touched on, each of us had significant personal circumstances arise. Each of us were unable to attend a handful of meetings during this third assignment but every time we were all so accommodating to one another. We caught one another up, delegated tasks or helped each other finish tasks. It was a great feeling that we all knew we had everyone's backs no matter what came up.

Perhaps each of us have had previous negative experiences working within groups, however we were all very surprised at the high quality and standard of work we all produced together. From this experience, collectively, each of us have realised that with a great bunch of people, we really can develop an innovative project. As a group, we had nil disagreements, each of us were heard when pitching a new idea to the team and have really enjoyed this experience collectively.

One surprising aspect that each of us learnt about one another, and quite possibly learnt about ourselves. Jeremy only ever submitted a perfect piece of work. Every ‘t’ was crossed and every ‘i’ was dotted. A perfectionist and one that takes such pride in everything he created for this and the previous assignment. Something else that we each learnt from Jeremy, if Jeremy wasn’t stressing, then we all knew not to stress.

Jacqui is an incredible html troubleshooter, there was always something that could be improved, and Jacqui would find out how, even if she was troubleshooting until 4am. 4am the following day, after that day.

Renee can spot an error from a mile away; she even edits her own edits and then re-edits those edits. English-US content couldn’t get past her. We think she created errors herself and then fixed those too.

Jarrad can create absolutely anything. Anything! We also think he learnt this about himself too. Jarrad approaches each task by nipping them in the bud. Simultaneously, he worked on every single task. If his raspberry pi arrived on time, he and Will would have already been selling HydroKnow to the fire brigades. And probably to New Zealand. And maybe Canada too.

We all still have no idea why Will is studying to be a Plumber. We all think he has chosen the wrong career. Very talented young man that we all admired. We said Jacqui was and is the backbone of this project, but Will’s idea brought us all together.

And, in summation, that is why, together, we all made an incredible team.
